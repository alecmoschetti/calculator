How To Use:

  1. One calculation at a time

  2. AC will clear the calculation in process

  3. CE acts as a backspace key while inputing numbers

  4. After a single calculation, if you want to perform a different calculation not involving the answer just calculated, then press AC otherwise your last calculation will be involved with your next calculation

  5. The only way to enter negative numbers: +- key will change your current input to the opposite sign

  6. Clicking an opeartor like + - x / after inputing your calculation will be equivalent to pressing equal

  7. Selecting degree or radian will change how sin, cos, and tan are calculated

  8. Solo operands like x!, √, log, ln, sin, cos, tan will operate correctly no matter what order you select the number or operand

  9. Order matters on operands like y√x or ^

  10. The former must input the number by which root you want to use followed by y√x then the number to be performed on

  11. The latter, you enter your base number first followed by the ^ then the number that will be the exponent

  12. Randm will generate a pseudorandom number

  13. Keyboard support to come in the future
